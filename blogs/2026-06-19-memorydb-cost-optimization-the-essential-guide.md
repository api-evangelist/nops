Amazon MemoryDB for Redis provides microsecond read latency with durable writes via Multi-AZ transaction logs, but its pricing structure of node-based billing, data written charges, snapshot storage, and data transfer fees can escalate quickly. Cost overruns commonly stem from overprovisioned clusters, underutilized setups, and insufficient use of commitment discounts. This guide covers cost-reduction strategies including cluster right-sizing, Reserved Instances, data tiering, Valkey migration, lifecycle automation, and multi-account cost management.
