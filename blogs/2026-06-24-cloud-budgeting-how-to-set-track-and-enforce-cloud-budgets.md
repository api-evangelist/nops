The cloud was supposed to make infrastructure easier to scale and easier to budget. Instead, many businesses are now dealing with invoices that shift every month, costs buried across teams and services, and new AI workloads that constantly store, move, and process data. Cloud budgeting in 2026 is no longer a once-a-year finance exercise.
